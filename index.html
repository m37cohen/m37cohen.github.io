Our Mission

As cities expand and land prices soar, life in the downtown core is becoming unattainable for the average family. The resulting suburban sprawl creates a divide between our cities, its people, and the planet.

Our mission is to provide a sustainable urban lifestyle for modern families - a lifestyle at the intersection of density, affordability, and sustainability.

Our Concept

Our proposal is a dense urban infill model that places residents in communities surrounded by public transit, street networks, modern utilities, services and amenities. Choosing sites that satisfy these requirements and maintain affordability are integral to our vision.

Our Team

Team Toronto is comprised of faculty and students from Ryerson University, University of Toronto and Seneca College. We are an interdisciplinary team from Architecture, Engineering and Business. Together we have extensive knowledge and expertise in conducting research for cutting edge technology, designing sustainable houses, implementing novel theoretical systems and constructing advanced buildings.

The Competition

The Solar Decathlon in an international competition wherein participating teams design, build and operate a full-scale, entirely functional net-zero energy house. The competition aims to educate students, inform the public and accelerate the adoption of energy-efficient products.
