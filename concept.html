solarBLOCK

solarBLOCK

Our design proposal is solarBLOCK, a single family dwelling that is modular and solar-powered, and can be built offsite, shipped and easily re-assembled. Currently in the detailed design and prototyping phase, solarBLOCK will begin construction in the summer of 2017.

Why?

In 2014, Canadian buildings and personal vehicles emitted a combined total of 167.2 megatonnes of CO2 equivalent, and according to the 2011 census, 57% of the population of the GTA lived in suburbs. Slow adoption of green design in addition to suburban sprawl have led to unsustainable housing developments emanating from the city. Our design proposal seeks to address this growing issue and provide a compelling solution that will help pave the way for a greener future.

The Impact

The research experience, new products, and skills that are developed during this project will allow for improvements in energy technologies and residential design as a whole. This will allow Canada to further its advanced energy technology sector, and improve its position in the global marketplace as the world begins its shift from fossil fuels, while allowing for a change in the design of our residential neighborhoods.
