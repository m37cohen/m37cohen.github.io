Overview

Our proposal is a dense urban infill model that places residents in inner city communities that are already well served by existing service infrastructure, public transit, parks, schools, cultural and community amenities. Choosing sites within biking and walking distance to jobs, schools and public transit and that maintain affordability and sense of community, are integral to our vision.

As a by-product of ad-hoc development and previous traffic engineering, many of Toronto's arterials that flow through these leafy neighbourhoods are flanked by viable, mixed-use and undervalued lots. These are the locations we seek to reinvigorate and restore with our midrise, mid-density proposal.


What makes our design unique?

Affordable

The home is designed to be affordable to produce. Everything from the walls to the roof will be pre-fabricated to facilitate rapid on-site assembly and to optimize transport in a shipping container.

Sustainable

The design optimizes passive and active strategies to maximize the performance of the building and achieve net zero energy units.

Stackable

The repeatable, stackable family home combats suburban sprawl, and provides the density needed to house our growing population.

Livable

Each home is designed to be comfortable and spacious, with an upstairs, downstairs, and outdoor living area.
