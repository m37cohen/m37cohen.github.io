Architecture

Our design revolves around the evolution of the single family home for a sustainable urban future. The traditional pieces of a house in the suburbs - private backyards, living spaces facing the outdoors, the porch – are all part of the architectural language of our homes. As such, we’ve transplanted these elements and modified them for their new urban environment, maintaining semi-public entryways, and creating spacious balconies populated with living plants.

The traditional gabled roof has also been modernised, sheathed in solar panels, it is now a reflective energy generator, folding over the south façade until it reaches the ground. This creates a continuous solar sheathing which transitions elegantly into the stacked house design.

The remainder of the building is clad in a rain screen of Canadian wood planks, even wrapping around to the northern roof. Using heat treated wood makes the facades, porch and patios easy to maintain in even the harshest Canadian winters, differentiate our design from the brick houses which already fill our suburbs.

The gable and overall form of our design are reminiscent of traditional houses, but it’s the details which mark it as contemporary architecture. Considerations such as a recessed soffit and drainage in the roof allow for clean connections, free of the typical soffits and visual clutter found in the suburbs.
