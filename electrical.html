Electrical Systems

Traditional buildings use decentralized control to regulate room temperatures and domestic hot water. This generates the desired temperatures, but since each system is controlled independently of each other, the dynamics of their interactions are ignored which leads to inefficiencies. Team [insert name] will be using a novel centralized Model Predictive Control (MPC) strategy based on work from Ryerson University [1]. This method is innovative since it requires a completely integrated mathematical model of the solar panels, HVAC system, domestic hot water, building layout, and building materials. Such models are unique to each building and can be difficult to properly refine.

Our building control system will use an adaptive control strategy to iteratively learn the building parameters. This iterative learning will allow a more precise and accurate control of the mechanical system. To our knowledge, the combination of a MPC strategy with adaptive control is a unique approach to building control systems.

In order to have a centralized control approach, a communication protocol between all of the interconnected systems needs to be implemented. Team [insert name] will be using the Modbus standard for communication of the actuators, controllers, and sensors in the building. Since off -the-shelf MPC controllers do not exist on the market, ECOstudio will need to design a dedicated control server. This server will handle the calculations for the MPC optimization, and relay the control information to the local controllers. A web server will also be implemented to allow the residents the ability to remotely monitor and control various aspects of the home.

In order to give the residents the suburban lifestyle they desire, the home will carry full size Energy Star appliances. This is in contradiction with most of the smaller appliances found in stacked condo dwellings in downtown Toronto. The home will also have LED lighting throughout the house to reduce energy consumption.
